01 // The Why

Racing is not math.
It is violence.

I chose this project because Formula 1 is the ultimate paradox. It is a sport defined by precision engineering, yet decided by pure chaos. A millimeter of tire wear, a sudden gust of wind at Silverstone, or a safety car in Jeddah can shatter the most robust strategy.

Traditional statistical models fail here because they assume a linear world. They cannot account for the "Ghost in the Machine." This project is my attempt to quantify that uncertaintyâ€”to embrace the randomness rather than ignore it. By combining hard data with probabilistic simulation, we don't just predict who wins; we predict the texture of the race itself.

02 // The Approach

From 1950 to Tomorrow.

We didn't just fit a line to a dataset. We built a comprehensive simulation engine that learns from over 70 years of racing heritage.

Historical Mining

Utilizing a massive dataset spanning 1950-2024. We deployed custom Selenium scrapers to extract granular track data and historical weather patterns that standard APIs miss.

Feature Alchemy

We engineered 20+ proprietary features and used RFECV (Recursive Feature Elimination) to isolate the signal from the noise, feeding an XGBoost Regressor tuned via Optuna.

Monte Carlo Engine

We simulate every race 1,000 times. By injecting a specific "Chaos Factor" into our predictions, we generate probability distributions that account for crashes, failures, and miracles.
