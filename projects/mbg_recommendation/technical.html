AI-Driven Regional Investment Planning

Utilizing spectral clustering to identify provinces with high agricultural potential and lagging economic development.

View Methodology

Project Overview

This project introduces an AI-based recommendation system for the Makan Bergizi Gratis (MBG) program. By analyzing datasets from BMKG, BPN, and BPS, our goal is to pinpoint provinces that exhibit strong agricultural potential but are currently experiencing lagging economic development. We employ unsupervised learning, specifically spectral clustering, due to the absence of predefined target labels and the necessity of incorporating geographical proximity into the model.

Feature Set Used

The model utilizes a set of 18 carefully selected features to capture a holistic view of each province's demographic, economic, and agricultural landscape.

Population

Total number of people.

Population Density

Population per square kilometer.

Area of Land

Total land area, both usable and unusable.

Area of Wetlands

Total area of land used for crops.

Regional GDP per Capita

Total value of goods produced per region.

Farmer Organization Rate

How much organized the farmers in a province are.

Farmer's Terms of Trade

An index measuring farmer's welfare.

Temperature

Average ambient temperature.

Farmer over Population

Ratio of farmers to the total population.

Local Direct Investment Dominance

How much of the total investment is made by local investors.

Humidity

Average ambient humidity.

Price-to-Self Sustainability Ratio

The average price over the aggregated sustainability of all provinces.

Soybean Yield Gap

The gap to the most productive province's soybean yield.

Paddy Yield Gap

The gap to the most productive province's paddy yield.

Layer Yield Gap

The gap to the most productive province's layer yield.

Rainfall Per Area

How much rain falls per unit area.

Rainfall Optimal Gap

Deviation from optimal rainfall levels.

Temperature Optimal Gap

Deviation from optimal temperature levels.

Commodity Basket Index

An index for the average price of all commodities sold.

Self Sustainability Ratio

Ratio between total consumption of a commodity to its production, per province.

Data Imputation Strategy

Missing data for certain provinces were handled using targeted imputation strategies to maintain data integrity and relevance.

New Papuan Provinces expand_more

Data for newer Papuan provinces were imputed using values from their parent province to ensure historical and geographical consistency.

DKI Jakarta expand_more

Missing values for DKI Jakarta were imputed using data from other similar highly urbanized provinces (Bali, Banten, etc.), reflecting its unique economic profile.

Kalimantan Provinces expand_more

Imputation for Kalimantan provinces was performed by taking the mean value across all provinces on the island of Kalimantan.

Sumatera Provinces expand_more

For provinces in Sumatera, missing data was filled using the mean value calculated from all other provinces on the island.

Clustering Algorithm

The accumulated dataset is then fed into a Machine Learning algorithm. For this, we used unsupervised learning, because there is no valid or 'official' result that indicates whether or not a province is worth investing for. To put into more details, we use Graph-Based Clustering, which is often known as 'Spectral Clustering'. This approach is due to the fact that nearby provinces share common traits, and investing in 1 province should lead in an increase of investment opportunity for the surrounding provinces.

Spectral clustering is a graph-based method that uses the Laplacian matrix and its eigenvectors to map the relational structure between data points—in this case, provinces. It is highly effective for this project because geographically close provinces often share similar climatic and economic characteristics, a nuance that traditional clustering methods might miss.

The algorithm outputs clusters of provinces with similar economic, climatic, and agricultural traits, forming a robust basis for targeted investment recommendations.

Cluster Interpretation

Cluster interpretation was not performed using PCA to avoid information loss. Instead, an XGBoost model was trained on the full feature set with cluster labels as the target.

The resulting feature importance scores, combined with domain knowledge, were used to interpret and profile each cluster. This method identifies the key drivers for each cluster, allowing us to determine which groups exhibit high agricultural potential alongside economic underperformance.

From this result, we can also deduce that the algorithm successfully put emphasize on the more important things, such as the amount of farmers, the amount of people, things relating to self-sustainability ratio (balance between consumption and production), and lastly the index representing the welfare of farmers.

Conclusion & Impact

By leveraging spectral clustering and interpreting results with feature importance analysis, this project provides a data-driven framework for regional investment planning. The insights generated assist policymakers in identifying priority regions, ensuring the Makan Bergizi Gratis program's resources are allocated effectively.
