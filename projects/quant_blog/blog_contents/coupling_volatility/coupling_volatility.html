Theoretical Framework

This analysis draws parallels between statistical mechanics and market behavior. We define three core metrics that govern the system:

Coupling Mass

$$ m_{eff}(t) $$

Calculated as the mean squared pairwise difference across all assets.

High Mass: Assets move independently (Diversified).
Low Mass: Assets move together (Systemic Risk).

Instability Temperature

$$ T(t) = \frac{1}{m_{eff}(t) + \epsilon} $$

Inverse of coupling mass. Represents market "heat" or chaos level.

High T: Unstable/Chaotic (Divergence).
Low T: Synchronized (Risk-on/Crash).

Market Volatility Measure

$$ \sigma(t) = \text{std}(\mathbf{r}_t) \text{ smoothed over 20 days} $$

Calculated as the standard deviation across all four assets at each time point, then smoothed with a 20-day rolling window. This captures systemic market turbulence rather than individual asset volatility.

High σ: Entire market is fluctuating wildly (shown as solid red background).
Low σ: Calm markets (transparent red, nearly invisible).

Critical Insights: The Paradoxes

Modern Portfolio Theory assumes stable correlations between assets. This analysis reveals a fatal flaw: correlations spike to 1 precisely when you need diversification most.

MARCH 2020 - COVID CRASH
Complete Coupling Collapse

All four assets fell simultaneously. Gold (traditional safe haven) initially declined 12%. Bitcoin crashed 50% in days. S&P 500 and JKSE plummeted in lockstep.

Result: A "diversified" portfolio of these 4 assets would have provided zero protection.

KEY IMPLICATION

Traditional buy-and-hold diversification is regime-dependent. Portfolios must be dynamically rebalanced based on coupling metrics. When m_eff drops (synchronization increases), risk management requires:

  • • Position size reduction across all assets
  • • Migration to truly uncorrelated strategies (vol arbitrage, market neutral)
  • • Tail risk hedging (options, VIX futures)

Conclusions

The physics-inspired framework reveals that financial markets exhibit thermodynamic-like behavior, with distinct hot (chaotic) and cold (synchronized) phases.

  • Market coupling is highly time-variant, invalidating static correlation assumptions.
  • Crisis periods exhibit extreme synchronization, destroying diversification benefits.
  • Bitcoin's role is evolving from pure speculation to an alternative asset class.
  • Geographic diversification (IHSG) provided better protection than Bitcoin pre-2023.
  • Volatility and coupling are distinct phenomena—high volatility ≠ high coupling necessarily.

"This analysis challenges the fundamental assumption of Modern Portfolio Theory that asset correlations are stable. Portfolios must be dynamically adjusted based on market regime detection."
